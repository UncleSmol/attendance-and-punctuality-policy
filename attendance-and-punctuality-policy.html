Attendance and Punctuality Policy

Purpose

This policy establishes guidelines for attendance and punctuality to maintain a productive, reliable, and professional environment. Consistent attendance and punctuality are essential for Villa Roma Hotelâ€™s service standards and contribute to a seamless guest experience and effective training for students.

Scope

This policy applies to all Villa Roma Hotel employees, including permanent staff, temporary staff, and trainees from Nkangala TVET College.

Policy Guidelines

1. Attendance Expectations

  • Regular Attendance: All employees and trainees are expected to attend their scheduled shifts or training sessions consistently and fulfill all work or training responsibilities.
  • Shift Adherence: Team members should work their entire scheduled shifts. Leaving early or arriving late without prior approval from a supervisor is discouraged.
  • Absenteeism: Absenteeism disrupts operations and impacts team morale. Employees are expected to keep unplanned absences to a minimum.

2. Punctuality Standards

  • Start of Shift: Employees and trainees must be at their assigned workstations and ready to begin work by the start of their scheduled shift.
  • Breaks and Meals: Follow designated break and meal times and return to work promptly. Extending breaks without permission is considered a punctuality issue.
  • Late Arrivals: Frequent tardiness is disruptive and will be monitored. Three or more instances of lateness within a month may result in corrective action.

3. Reporting Absences and Lateness

  • Notification Requirement: Employees or trainees who cannot attend a shift or expect to be late must inform their supervisor at least 2 hours before the start of their shift or as soon as possible for unforeseen situations.
  • Acceptable Notification Methods: Notifications should be given via approved communication channels (e.g., phone call, text, or email) to a supervisor or designated contact.
  • Documentation for Absences: Medical certification may be required for absences of two or more consecutive days due to illness. Additional documentation may be required for other types of leave.

4. Types of Absences

  • Planned Leave: This includes vacation leave, family leave, and other pre-approved absences. Employees and trainees are expected to request planned leave at least 14 days in advance for proper scheduling.
  • Sick Leave: Employees and trainees should notify their supervisor as early as possible if sick. Medical documentation may be required after multiple days of absence.
  • Unplanned Absences: Unforeseen emergencies or issues should be communicated promptly. Excessive unplanned absences may result in corrective action.

5. Consequences of Non-Compliance

  • Corrective Actions: Frequent absenteeism, tardiness, or failure to notify supervisors may result in verbal or written warnings, performance reviews, suspension, or, for severe or repeated violations, termination.
  • Escalation: Continued non-compliance will be escalated to the HR department for formal review and action, which may include adjustments to shift schedules or changes in responsibilities.

6. Exemptions and Special Considerations

  • Emergency Situations: Recognizing that emergencies arise, the policy allows for flexibility in cases of unexpected illness, family emergencies, or other unavoidable situations. Employees are encouraged to communicate openly with supervisors about these circumstances.
  • Training Program Adjustments: For trainees from Nkangala TVET College, certain academic requirements may occasionally conflict with scheduled shifts. Such conflicts should be communicated with as much advance notice as possible to allow for scheduling adjustments.

Policy Review and Updates

This policy will be reviewed annually to adapt to any changes in operational needs, industry standards, or workforce dynamics. Updates will be communicated to all employees and trainees through official channels.

Acknowledgment

All employees and trainees are required to read, understand, and acknowledge this policy as a condition of employment or participation in the Villa Roma training program.
